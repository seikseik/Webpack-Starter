How customers perceive Whirlpool?

What‚Äôs ABSA?

Aspect-based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA) is a technique for categorizing data by aspects and identifying the sentiment attached to each one. By associating specific sentiments to different attributes of a product or service, ABSA can be used to analyze customer feedback, reviews or conversations.

Why aspects are important?

It is important to note that ABSA differs from other sentiment analysis techniques in the presence of aspects. By considering aspects, it is possible to extract meaningful insights from data. Aspects can range from specific products to features or whole categories. With ABSA, you can track how customers react to and feel about your business. ABSA is scalable because you don't have to manually classify thousands of conversations or reviews. This allows businesses to automatically analyze massive amounts of data both in detail and in real-time.

From aspects to topics

However, the level of aspects is not enough. Since they are extracted directly from the text, the aspects can be any word or expression and their number is too high to be able to have a global overview. Moreover, the words expressed by the user are not necessarily consistent with the metrics that the business wants to monitor. This is why, using another machine learning model, we map the aspects into a topic level, composed of a few macro topics that contain slightly more detailed subtopics. In this way, the topic level is stable over time and allows a comparative analysis even over different periods.

The project

To help Whirlpool we collected 84K reviews from customers about the quality of the customer service in Italy. These reviews were left after customers received assistance from Whirlpool technicians or other customer service people consequent to a failure of a product. We built a dataset that took into consideration 4 years of activity, from 2017 to 2020 to ensure a time horizon with consistent data. From this dataset, we were able to identify relevant business aspects such as brand names, products, or intervention quality. The following are the key business insights that we found.

overview

Dataset composition

The reviews come from a questionnaire made up of some structured questions and a free text question. We isolated only the open question for the Italian customers and used that text as input to our model.

The fields of the starting dataset are the ID of the review, the date, the text of the review and the product family involved in the repair.

The ABSA model works in two steps: in the first we extract aspect-sentiment pairs in an unsupervised way, in the second we map the aspect to a subtopic that represents a category that is easier to interpret at a business level. In turn, the subtopic is contained within a wider topic which is an even more generic category and constitutes the thematic area to be monitored.

4453 aspects

About the model

To carry out this project we used a pipeline divided into two steps. For the first, we extracted the aspect-sentiment pairs with an end-to-end Transformer model, in which the aspects are extracted as keywords from the text and are not defined a priori (https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.00883). We used a dataset available on Evalita for a first fine-tuning to teach the model the task and a second dataset of 200 observations created manually to specialize the model in the context of these surveys.

For the second step, we did a semantic matching of aspects to topics. The challenge in this case is that the context of the sentence affects the match as an aspect can be within several different topics (for example "price" can be "product cost", "call cost", "intervention cost",. ..). We then created a dataset of 500 observations to fine-tune a BERT model using the input "{sentence} [SEP] {aspect}". This allowed us to have a mapping of the aspects consistent with the semantics of the sentence.
